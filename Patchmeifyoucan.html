Role: Game Designer

About the Project

This is a fast-paced first-person shooter where you play as a voodoo tailor on a mission to stitch back together and purify your runaway creations, now scattered across eerie amusement park attractions.

The gameplay features high-speed, fluid movement inspired by classic titles. Players are encouraged to stay mobile, chaining actions quickly while navigating levels filled with misbehaving creations. This fast-paced rhythm keeps the action engaging and constantly evolving.

Thanks to the dedication and synergy of the entire team, we were able to deliver a fun experience. Each member contributed with passion and creativity, allowing us to craft a game that not only plays well but also offers a distinct visual and thematic identity.

This academic project was also a major opportunity for personal growth. I focused particularly on combat design, and more specifically on the development of shooting systems — refining weapon mechanics, player feedback, and pacing to support the game's energetic feel.

Design Breakdown

Weapon Mechanics

  • Stitching gun combines ranged combat with melee finishing moves
  • Single projectile (needle) that must return to weapon before next shot
  • Shooting available while sliding, jumping, or crouching
  • "E" key to pull hooked enemies toward player
  • Movement slowed during shooting and hooking actions

Detailed mechanics documented in GDD

Enemy Design

  • Puppets wander with dynamic pathfinding
  • Dual conical fields of view for visual detection
  • Spherical hearing range for audio detection
  • Escape behavior with environmental interaction
  • Inspired by Ape Escape movement patterns

Complete AI system detailed in GDD

Feedback Systems

  • Visual hit indicators for successful hits
  • Audio cues for weapon actions and enemy reactions
  • Visual effects for player states (crouching, etc.)
  • Dynamic enemy visual feedback
  • Comprehensive UI feedback system

Full feedback systems documented in GDD
