The Sun Vanished

Solo project

Objective: Fast Prototyping

About the Project

In this project, I aimed to replicate some combat mechanics inspired by Souls-like games. It was a nearly three-month-long project where I focused entirely on combat design. I handled animations, sound effects, hit feedback, and implemented enemy AI behavior to create responsive and challenging encounters.

Additionally, I worked on Niagara effects to enhance the visual impact of combat interactions. As a learning resource, I followed a training video on YouTube, which helped guide me through some technical steps while allowing me to adapt and customize the systems to fit my design goals.

More about the project

Project Implementation

  • Combo system with 4 different attack variations.
  • Blink ability with Niagara effects.
  • Root motion animations for precise movement and attack control.
  • Enemy AI with custom health systems and unique attack behaviors.
  • Stealth attack mechanic from behind.
  • Separate run and walk animations depending on whether the sword is equipped or sheathed.
  • Boss enemy with ability speed changes triggered at specific health thresholds.
  • Lock-on camera system inspired by Souls-like games.

Obstacles and Difficulties

One of the main challenges in this project was working with root motion animations, which often conflicted with my character's skeleton. I had to install Blender plugins, import the character, and rig it using a dedicated tool, making sure the animations were properly aligned and functional inside the engine.

Another issue I faced was managing the interaction between enemy health systems and the weapon's collider, to ensure the game could reliably detect when an enemy was being hit during combat.

Lessons Learned

  • Working with root motion animations and using Blender plugins for character rigging.
  • Implementing enemy AI with custom attack behaviors.
  • Integrate Niagara effects for gameplay feedback.
  • Exploring different methods to build combo systems.
  • Gaining deeper knowledge of state machines and animation blueprints in Unreal Engine.

Implementation Insights - Combat Systems

Key Learnings from Souls-like Combat Implementation

Developing combat mechanics inspired by Souls-like games taught me valuable lessons about player feedback and animation timing. The precise nature of these games required meticulous attention to detail in every interaction.

The most significant insight was understanding the importance of root motion animations for creating weighty and impactful combat. Properly implemented root motion gives attacks a sense of physical presence and momentum that traditional animation blending cannot achieve.

Another crucial learning was the balance between player control and animation commitment. Souls-like combat requires players to commit to their actions, which creates meaningful decisions about when to attack, dodge, or defend.

Finally, I discovered the value of gradual difficulty scaling through enemy AI. Starting with simple attack patterns and gradually introducing more complex behaviors helped players learn the combat system naturally.

Technical Challenge

AI Behavior and Animation Integration Insights

Developing enemy AI for a Souls-like project taught me valuable lessons about behavior tree design and animation integration. Enemies needed to feel challenging but fair, with telegraphed attacks that players could learn to recognize and counter.

One key insight was the importance of telegraphing attacks through animation. Each enemy attack needed clear wind-up animations that gave players time to react, creating a rhythm of observation and response.

I learned that boss design required special consideration. The boss enemy with ability speed changes at health thresholds created memorable moments that tested player adaptation skills.

The integration of stealth mechanics added variety to combat encounters. Allowing players to approach situations differently created emergent gameplay possibilities beyond direct confrontation.
